MECHANICAL ENGINEERING

Build machines that build machines.

Introduction


Mechanical engineering is a four-year BTech programme that teaches the students the basic understanding and knowledge of how heavy tools and machinery work. A student pursuing a mechanical engineering programme will acquire knowledge about designing automobiles, electric motors, aircraft and other heavy vehicles. Mechanical engineers design everything from new batteries, athletic equipment to medical devices and from personal computers, air conditioners, automobile engines to electric power plants. These engineers also design machines that produce these innovations. The scope of mechanical engineering in India and abroad is huge for every aspect of our lives are touched by mechanical engineering in one way or the other. Spanning multiple industries, the career opportunities for students pursuing the programme is huge. Mechanical engineering is offered by various engineering colleges in India at the undergraduate as well postgraduate levels, where students are awarded BTech and MTech degrees respectively.

Career options


  • Mechanical Engineer
  • Design Engineer
  • Assistant Mechanical Engineer
  • Purchase and Quality Control Executive
  • Service Engineer
  • Instructional Designer
  • Mechanical Designer

Higher studies


BTech graduates generally opt for an M.tech while there is a wide range of trending and professional Engineering courses for the future that is available across the globe. Below we have detailed some of the prominent courses after Mechanical Engineering from diverse fields of study that you can discover:

MBA


MBA after BTech is a great choice for those wanting to gain imperative managerial skills and knowledge of handling a business. For MBA for Engineers, there are plentiful specializations available like MBA IT, MBA Entrepreneurship, etc, and aim to impart students with a detailed knowledge of their chosen engineering specialization along with building their own business from scratch.


PG Courses in Piping Design & Engineering


Associated with Mechanical Engineering, Piping Design aims to facilitate a detailed analysis of physical piping layout formulated within a process plant or a structure constructed for commercial purposes. Various engineering experts and mentors also endorse the fact Piping Engineering and Design is the best option when it comes to choosing courses after Mechanical Engineering. This course is relevant to Mechanical Engineering as it carries most of its concepts. There are lucrative job prospects available in the field of piping design and internationally acclaimed universities are offering this course with a healthy educational environment.


MBA in Operations Management


To ensure smooth operations, companies hire operations experts for the proper functioning of the company. With the logical aptitude that makes an engineer, in this course, they can treat the overall working of the company as a machine and ensure a boost in productivity.


MBA in Supply Chain Management


Many B.Tech students opt for this course after completing Mechanical Engineering. They ensure the management of the supply chain of goods and services of the company happens smoothly and efficiently.


Master of Engineering in Tool Design


Tool Design is concerned with the assessment, planning, formulation, design, building as well as application of different types of tools, methodologies as well as processes required to boost manufacturing productivity. Masters of engineering and tool design is one of the sought-after courses after Mechanical Engineering. There are globally prestigious universities around the globe offering master’s as well as PG diploma courses in this field.

Average salary


A mid-career Mechanical Engineer with 4-9 years of experience earns an average salary of ₹4.5 Lakhs per year, while an experienced Mechanical Engineer with 10-20 years of experience earns an average salary of ₹7.7 Lakhs per year.
